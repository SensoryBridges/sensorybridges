Simple for the wearer. Powerful for the team.

The student wears a Samsung Galaxy Watch. The watch quietly observes. The team gets the data they need — without interrupting the student's day.

🎙️

Continuous Voice Volume Awareness

The watch measures the wearer's own voice volume continuously and unobtrusively throughout the day. When volume exceeds the wearer's personal range, a gentle vibration provides a private cue only they can feel. No one else in the room knows.

📊

Vocal & Sensory Data Collection

Six sensor streams deliver objective data across vocal, movement, and environmental channels. Event counts, timestamps, durations, and acoustic measurements — everything a professional needs for documentation, without a clipboard.

🔐

Privacy by Design

No audio is ever recorded, stored, or transmitted. The watch measures sound level only — like a sound level meter on the wrist. Only wellness observation labels leave the device. Nothing identifiable. Nothing exploitable.

👤

Personal to Each Wearer

The device learns each wearer's own voice and personal range. Thresholds adjust to the individual — not a one-size-fits-all number. The device distinguishes the wearer's voice from background sound.

🏫

Built for Schools

Runs on Samsung Galaxy Watch in freestanding mode — no paired phone needed. Managed and secured through enterprise device management. Designed for all-day wear in a school environment.

💚

Dignity First

No public alerts. No visible signals to classmates. No adult having to say "use your inside voice" for the hundredth time. Just a quiet, private tap on the wrist.

The Brooks Band™ Dashboard — AVVOF™ Copyrighted Observation System

IDEA Assistive Technology & Funding Pathways

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), assistive technology devices and services must be made available to children with disabilities if required as part of their IEP.

  • IDEA Part B funds can be used for assistive technology devices and services listed in a student's IEP
  • Title I and Title IV-A grants support technology that improves educational outcomes for students with disabilities
  • State-level assistive technology lending libraries and loan programs (available in both Tennessee and Georgia)
  • School district special education budgets allocated for assistive technology procurement

All funding pathway availability depends on the individual student's IEP and the district's procurement process. Sensory Bridges provides documentation support — all educational and service decisions are made by qualified licensed professionals.

Privacy, Compliance & Policies

Privacy Policy

Effective: March 31, 2026 | Last Updated: March 31, 2026

Sensory Bridges, Inc. ("Company," "we," "us") is committed to protecting the privacy of all users, including students, families, educators, and website visitors.

1. Information We Collect

Website Forms: When you submit a form on this website, we collect the information you voluntarily provide — name, email, organization, role, and any details you share.

Brooks Band Device Data: Voice volume level (dB), ambient sound level (dB), event timestamps, event durations, and wellness observation labels. No audio is ever recorded. No speech content, words, conversations, photographs, or location data (in school deployment). All data linked to a de-identified code — never a student name.

Website Analytics: This website uses no cookies, no tracking pixels, no analytics scripts, and no third-party advertising code.

2. How We Use Information

  • Respond to inquiries and process form submissions
  • Send product updates (only if opted in)
  • Evaluate partnership and investment opportunities
  • Provide wellness observation data to authorized users in defined roles
  • Support IEP documentation and educational team collaboration

3. Data Transmission & Storage

Observation data syncs via encrypted connection (TLS 1.3) and is encrypted at rest (AES-256). No data is transmitted to any cloud service, third party, or external server outside the school's platform instance. Website form submissions are stored in Airtable with access restricted to authorized team members.

4. Data Retention

Device data retained for the current IEP cycle (typically one academic year) and automatically purged at end of retention period. Data can be deleted at any time by the authorized adult or school administrator.

5. We Will Never

  • Sell, rent, or trade your personal information
  • Share your information with advertisers or data brokers
  • Use data collected under the COPPA school exception for any commercial purpose
  • Use your data beyond this policy without explicit consent

6. Your Rights

Request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal information at any time: privacy@sensorybridges.com or info@sensorybridges.com.

FERPA Compliance

All observation data is classified as an education record under FERPA (20 U.S.C. § 1232g) when used in a school setting. The school district is the data controller. Sensory Bridges operates as a "school official" under a FERPA-compliant Data Processing Agreement (DPA).

Parents and eligible students retain all FERPA rights including the right to inspect, review, request amendment, and request deletion of all observation data. No data is disclosed to any third party without prior written parent consent or an applicable FERPA exception.

Tennessee: Student data additionally protected under the Data Accessibility, Transparency and Accountability Act (Tenn. Code Ann. § 49-1-701 et seq.) and the Student Online Personal Protection Act.

Georgia: Student data privacy governed by the Georgia Student Data Privacy, Accessibility, and Transparency Act (O.C.G.A. § 20-2-324.2), which requires comprehensive data security programs and prohibits targeted advertising based on student data.

COPPA Compliance

Under COPPA's school exception (15 U.S.C. §§ 6501–6506), the school may consent on behalf of the parent for educational purposes only.

  • No data collected under the school exception is used for any commercial purpose
  • No data shared with third parties for non-educational purposes
  • No persistent identifiers collected from students
  • Parents retain the right to review, request deletion of, and refuse further collection at any time

Sensory Bridges is designed to comply with the 2025 COPPA amendments (full compliance deadline April 22, 2026), including formal written security programs and explicit parental consent requirements.

Cookie & Tracking Policy

This website uses no cookies, no tracking pixels, no analytics scripts, and no third-party advertising code. Form submissions are processed through Airtable (airtable.com/privacy). The production platform will use only essential session cookies for user authentication.

Opt-Out & Data Control

Any sensor stream can be independently disabled at any time by the authorized adult. The device can be completely deactivated at any time. All data collection ceases immediately upon deactivation. Previously collected data can be deleted upon request. Contact: privacy@sensorybridges.com
